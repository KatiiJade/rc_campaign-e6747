The only Academy in the [[Atlas/Radiant Citadel\|Radiant Citadel]]; The Radiant Academy boasts their status as the best across the planes. Due to the collaboration from famous scholars across the Civilisations, the Academy offers excellent research programs across many fields. Including, but not limited to: arcane studies, archaeology, art, business, culture, ecology, engineering, history, medicine, music, politics, science, and technology.

Many of the Radiant Academy's discoveries have had great life changing improvements and advancements in the Citadel's history. 

## Main Objective
Research either a particular field or various fields of study.
 
## Assignments
Scholarly missions can include adventurous archaeology, arcane research, investigative ecology, historical research, radical engineering, and exploration.

Researchers can choose to do Research Projects or the Academy also advertises adhoc jobs that can get them extra funding.

## Benefits
- Compensation for work done on behalf of the Academy.
- Academy documentation that grants you access to other academic circles and organisations.
- Letters of introduction and travelling papers if your work requires them.
- Access to resources and locations often off limits to others such as dangerous relics, magic items, spell books, literature, ruins etc.
- Ability to delegate research to a colleague, contact or research assistant (Characters may need to cover this expense).
- Training opportunities for different languages, musical instruments, tools, and proficiencies. 
- Proficiency with one skill, tool or musical instrument of your choice.

## Researcher Roles
- Field Researcher - similar to Scholar, but is more combat oriented. 
- Financier - seeks funds to pay for academic expeditions. High charisma, deception, intimidation and persuasion. Bards and rogues suited. Often charlatan or noble backgrounds.
- Research Assistant - seeking to graduate, get published, or make their academic mark. Could be from any class or background. 
- Scholar - high intelligence focus on learning and research. More often non-combatant. Can include wizards, clerics and monks. Sage, acolyte or hermit backgrounds. Proficiency in arcana, history, investigation, nature, or religion.
